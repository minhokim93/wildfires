00 — Research Context

Wildfires represent one of the most dynamic and destructive natural hazards on Earth. Their behavior is shaped by an intricate interplay of climate, ecology, topography, and human settlement — and all of these factors are shifting simultaneously.

This research program addresses wildfire risk from a multiscale perspective: understanding how global fire regimes are evolving, how synchronous fire weather is expanding hazard windows, how extreme fires emerge in unexpected geographies, how simulations can improve operational response, and how risk is shared and mitigated at the community scale.

The six projects presented here span from global pyrogeographic analysis using machine learning down to parcel-level responsibility networks for neighborhood fire risk mitigation.

— Six Projects · Global to Local
01
🌍 Global Scale · Machine Learning

Dynamic Evolution of Pyrogeography

Using unsupervised ML on 23 years of satellite fire data to cluster global fire regimes into pyromes and track how they transition and intensify over time.

Explore project →
 02
🌡️ Global Scale · Climate

Dynamic Evolution of Fire Weather Risk

Quantifying synchronous fire weather days (SFW) using the Fire Weather Index and assessing the conditional probability of fire occurrence under extreme weather.

Explore project →
 03
🇰🇷 Regional Scale · Case Study

Are Extreme Fires Unprecedented or Unexpected?

Benchmarking the March 2025 South Korea wildfire — the nation's most destructive — against global fast-fire databases and analyzing the anomalous climate conditions.

Explore project →
 04
🔥 Local Scale · Modeling

Fighting Fires with Wildfire Simulations

Coupling Cell2Fire with WindNinja to produce spatially-explicit, downscaled wind fields. Applied to the 2025 Los Angeles Palisades Fire first 32-hour spread.

Explore project →
 05
🗺️ Operational · Decision Support

Fighting Fires with Fire Potential Risk Networks

Automating fire potential polygon (FPP) generation via hydrology-inspired modeling and building suppression strategy networks. Deployed with Catalan Fire Service.

Explore project →
 06
🏘️ Neighborhood Scale · Community

Mitigating Risk as Shared Responsibility

Rethinking defensible spaces as overlapping buffers and developing spatial responsibility networks that quantify shared and owed risk between neighboring parcels.
